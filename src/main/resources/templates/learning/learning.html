Account Balance

Your account balance is the amount of money you have after adding all your business and stock earnings. You will begin with $10,000 and may use this money to buy inventory, addons, and stock. Note: On your first quiz attempt, you will earn an extra $500 towards your balance for each correct answer! See below to reach the business and stock quiz pages. Each completed quiz earns up to $2,500. Good luck!

Profit

Profit is the amount of money you keep after you subtract the amount of money you spent on buying inventory from the earnings from selling lemonades. For example, if the cost of lemonade ingredients is $1 and you sell each lemonade for $4, then you make a profit of $3!

Inventory

Inventory is a complete list of the items you have available to make lemonades. For example, you need lemons, sugar, water, ice, and other fruits to make your lemonades. When you are running out of any one of these items, you must purchase more to add to your inventory so you can make more lemonades. Note: Water is free in Kid Invest, and you will never need to purchase it!

Transaction

A transaction occurs you buy or sell something. Each time you purchase inventory, this is shown on your business transactions page. When you buy stock, it is shown on your stock transactions page. Your daily sales will be shown on your sales page.

Popularity

The more popular your lemonade stand is, the more your customers will be willing to pay to try your lemonade. Higher prices mean more profit, and more profit means more money!

Addons

In this game, purchasing addons will increase the popularity of your lemonade stand. For example, if you purchase a radio, more customers will enjoy visiting your lemonade stand and pay more for your lemonades.

Invest

Investing means putting money, time, or effort into something to make a long term gain. You can invest time and money into your lemonade business and spend those profits on stocks that may increase in value over time.

Stock

A stock is a type of investment that represents an ownership piece or share of a company. Investors buy stocks that they think will go up in value over time and increase their profits. However, some companies lose value or go out of business, and investors may lose part or all of their investment. This is why it is important to buy stock in different companies instead of focusing on just one.

Market Price

A stock's market price is the price that it sells for at a given point in time. The market price increases and decreases throughout the trading day as investors buy and sell stocks. The market price rises if more people want to buy it and falls when people do not want to buy it.

Profiting From Your Stocks

In this game, you can profit from selling stocks at a price higher than what you paid for them. This is what it means to "buy-low and sell-high!"

Total Return

The total return is a measure of an investment's overall performance and growth over time. So if you buy shares and their value increases since the time that you purchased them, the difference will be your total return.

Investment Portfolio

An investment portfolio is a grouping of financial assets. In this case, your portfolio includes all the stocks that you currently own plus your bank account total.
